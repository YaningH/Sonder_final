112.2 FM COSMIC INSIGNIFICANCE

I get so wrapped up in my own life, thoughts, feelings, and ambitions that I forget there is a whole world out there of 7.7 billion lives just as real as mine.

“I’m unique but not special.”

The universe that surrounds us is vast, and we are so small. When we reflect on the vastness of the universe, our humdrum cosmic location, and the inevitable future demise of humanity, our lives can seem utterly insignificant.

Many philosophers assume that such worries about our significance reflect a banal metaethical confusion. They dismiss the very idea of cosmic significance. This, I argue, is a mistake. Worries about cosmic insignificance do not express metaethical worries about objectivity or nihilism, and we can make good sense of the idea of cosmic significance and its absence.
